--- id: oxidation-of-ammonia-with-platinum-catalyst mel_id: MEL000009 type: experiment experiment_name: Oxidation of ammonia with platinum catalyst category: burning reaction: 4NH3 + 5O2 =[Pt]= 4NO + 6H2O description: | Put a small amount of concentrated ammonia solution into a beaker. Take a platinum wire and make a construction that you can easily put inside the beaker. Heat the platinum wire in a flame until it glows red and put in the beaker. Ammonia is oxidized by oxygen on the platinum wire surface. The platinum wire acts as a catalyst in this reaction which generates a lot of heat to make the wire glowing red hot. explosion: 0 color_change: 0 fire: 1 precipitate: 0 gassing: 0 smoke: 0 catalyst: 1 phases: gas+solid trick: comments: safety: | Concentrated aqueous ammonia can cause burns and is irritating to the skin, eyes, and respiratory tract. Wear eye protection. The resulting nitrogen dioxide is an extremely toxic gas by inhalation. Avoid breathing it.
